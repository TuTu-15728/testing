Fire Risk Assessment (FRA)

A Fire Risk Assessment (FRA) is a professional examination to analyze potential fire hazards and risks in a premise, ensuring safety and protection.

Fire Risk Management

Fire risk management involves identifying potential hazards and risks associated with fire in the premises. A comprehensive FRA helps eliminate or reduce these risks by implementing control measures. It’s essential to ensure the safety of all occupants and prevent any harm in the event of a fire emergency.

Why Do We Need a FRA?

  • It’s a legal requirement in India
  • Eliminate or reduce the risk of harm to everyone in or around the premises
  • Protect business interests—moral, social, and financial costs involved
  • Maintain a positive fire safety culture in an organization

Main Steps of Fire Risk Assessment

  1. Identify potential fire hazards
  2. Identify people at risk
  3. Evaluate, remove, reduce, and protect from risk
  4. Record, plan, inform, instruct, and train
  5. Review

When to Review a FRA?

Every 12 months

When layouts, occupants, and construction materials change

When the purpose of the building changes

How Does the Integration Work?

When a fire is detected, multiple systems work together automatically:

  • Fire detection triggers alarms and suppression systems.
  • HVAC dampers shut to contain smoke.
  • Smoke control directs smoke to designated areas.
  • Emergency lighting and PAVA systems guide evacuation.

Why is System Integration Important?

  • Simultaneous activation of all safety measures ensures fast responses.
  • Speedy evacuation and control during emergencies.
  • Protects assets and optimizes energy use.
  • Monitor all systems from a single interface.
  • Fully automatic operation reduces the risk of mistakes.

  • Encourages Serious Attention to Fire Alarms: Reinforces the importance of paying attention to fire alarms.
  • Prevents Panic: Helps occupants stay calm and adhere to the pre-decided evacuation plan during real emergencies.

Fire Safety Certificate (FSC) / Fire NOC

An official document issued by the fire safety authorities in India, the Fire Safety Certificate (FSC) acts as a No Objection Certificate (NOC), permitting a building or premise to operate legally and confirming compliance with fire safety regulations.

What is FSC?

The Fire Safety Certificate (FSC) ensures that a building is compliant with current fire safety laws and provides the necessary documentation for legal operation. It is an essential certificate for any establishment that wishes to operate without legal complications.

Legal Requirement

It’s mandatory for all organizations to possess an approved and valid FSC to operate legally.

Compliance

Ensures that the fire safety system meets current legislation and industry standards.

Insurance Requirement

Many insurance companies require a valid FSC for coverage on commercial and industrial properties.

Safety

Most importantly it is essential to provide maximum safety for people and property.

Step 1: Online Application

Submit an application online with details of the fire safety systems in the premises.

Step 2: Inspection

The fire department conducts an inspection to ensure all fire safety systems meet regulations.

Step 3: Issuance of FSC

After successful inspection, the Fire Safety Certificate (FSC) is issued by the local fire department.
